St. Mark's Lutheran Church in Wapakoneta, Ohio

St. Mark's offered me some of my first experiences in working with technology outside of fixing the wifi router at home. I began in December of 2017 in arguably one of the hardest positions that I had to face. They asked me if I could do the sound for our Christmas program with 4 people mic'd up and a handheld mic being passed around. I had to make sure no unnessecary sound was being picked up and that we got no feedback from microphones being too close to each other. Talk about being put through the gauntlet right away. I love a good challenge though, and the performance went perfectly. I ended up getting hooked and did this for the church while I was home until the pandemic hit and church became virtual. At that point, another sound tech and I worked to set up a bunch of livestreaming equipment all around the church so that online viewers could get an experience as close as possible to the real thing. We streamed for the summer, and then church returned to in person worship, but we decided to keep the program going. While I am not involved anymore, it did teach me several things about sound quality and management for big systems. This has been beneficial for me in other not similar but fun scenarios. For example, at Miami, my friends and I will go to the open skates which are hosted in Goggin Ice Center, Miami's hockey rink. The scorer's table has one of those big sound boards, and since there was no music playing, I was able to configure the machine and rig up my phone to play music for the other skaters and my friends.
